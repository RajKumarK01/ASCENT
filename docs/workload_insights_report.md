# Workload and Learning Correlation (Synthetic)

> Synthetic demonstration document. Figures are fabricated.

## Insights

- Employees with more than 20 meeting hours per week show lower study completion.
- Optimal completion appears when learners have 12–18 meeting hours and at least 15 focus hours per week.
- Honouring a learner's preferred learning slot (morning / afternoon / evening) improves adherence to study plans.

## Recommendation

- Schedule study blocks during focus-heavy periods rather than meeting-heavy ones.
- For capacity-constrained learners (>20 meeting hours/week), prefer shorter, condensed study sessions and avoid reminders during peak meeting windows.
- Treat work signals as supportive context only; never expose individual schedule detail in team-level reporting.
